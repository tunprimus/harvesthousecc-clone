Brief history

Driven by a vision of raising Models, empowering Saints and fulfilling Destinies, HarvestHouse Christian Centre (HCC) started as Oasis of Faith Christian Centre on 9th April 1992 at Bolajoko Nursery and Primary School, a small 10×12 feet room in the city of Ibadan with a membership of less than 15 people; most of whom were secondary school students. It later became Harvest International Church (HIC) in September 1996.

In the course of time, with more students from higher institutions of learning in attendance, HCC campus operations kicked off at the University of Ado-Ekiti (UNAD), and later at Ibadan Polytechnic and Ladoke Akintola University of Technology (LAUTECH) and Obafemi Awolowo University (OAU) and University of Ibadan.

In 1998, HCC moved her operations to the Bronx Club in Ibadan, later to 2nd Floor Iyaniwura House, Basorun in Ibadan in 1999 and then SDM Events Centre, Ibadan. HCC presently holds services at three different campuses in Ibadan — Alpha Cathedral (Police Bus Stop, Akobo, Ibadan); Metro Church (Mauve 21 Events Centre, Adeoyo Junction, MKO Abiola Way, Ring Road, Ibadan); and Life Centre (Bashmol Hall, along Holy Trinity Grammar School Road, Abayomi Estate, Old-Ife Road, Ibadan).

In September 2006, HCC Lagos was inaugurated under the pastoral care of Pastor Tunde-Success Osideko at the Tastee Fried Chicken eatery, Ogba, Lagos. The church has been experiencing additions in all her meetings. HCC Lagos now worships in a larger facility at the Novus Centre, 12K Nana Okuribido Street, Ismail Estate, Idiroko, Maryland, Lagos.

The church has also grown to have strong expressions in Abuja Nigeria (Merit House, Aguiyi Ironsi Street, Maitama, Abuja), South Africa, United Kingdom and North America.

HarvestHouse has been relevant to different categories of people through its various ministries like the Move Your World Conferences and the Vision House (Media Ministry).
